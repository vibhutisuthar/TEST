Medicine and Occupational Health


Dear employee*,
You recently scheduled a trip to a country where malaria is a health risk. Malaria can be serious and potentially fatal if not diagnosed and treated promptly. However, it is preventable. As ExxonMobil's priority is the safety and health of its employees, we want to ensure that travelers to malaria-risk areas are provided with the right information and, for non-immune travelers, appropriate medication. It is very important that all travelers to malaria-risk areas follow the Company's requirements to prevent malaria.

Before traveling to a malaria risk area, you must obtain a Malaria VISA through the Malaria web site. This process will verify that your awareness of malaria risks is current, that you have completed a MCCP Attestation and training (non-immune only), and for several destinations, that you have a malaria survival kit. If you arrive in country without a valid malaria visa, you will be requested to go though the malaria visa process before you are allowed to start your work activities. To obtain the Malaria VISA, click here http://emcorp.na.xom.com/imoh/Upstream_Health/public_health/Malaria/index.html and follow the instructions. After completing the process, print your personal Malaria VISA and carry it with you when traveling. The Malaria VISA displays an expiration date and must be renewed before it expires.

General health information about health risks and recommended health prevention measures is available on the ExxonMobil intranet site at http://emcorpapps.na.xom.com/Travax/index.html. Click on "Destination Information" and then click on the country to which you will be traveling.

However, this information does not replace the advice of a travel health consultant. Before you depart, please make an appointment with the nearest ExxonMobil clinic or contact your regional travel health consultant (travel consultant information is available on the ExxonMobil intranet site; http://emcorp.na.xom.com/imoh/Country_Contacts/Map_Page.htm ). You will receive information about health risks and how to manage them. Your health consultant will provide or give you a prescription for anti-malaria medication. If you visit an ExxonMobil clinic, you will also receive a travel convenience kit and an EMERS (emergency health contact) card. Your immunizations will also be reviewed to ensure that all recommended vaccines for your destination have been offered and are current.

As you know, the best treatment is prevention. Without protection, you might expose yourself to serious health risks. Even if you are familiar with the country you are traveling to, call the clinic. Preventive measures and recommendations change over time.

If you have any questions about the Malaria Prevention and Control Program, please contact your supervisor or a travel health consultant.

Bon voyage!

Medicine and Occupational Health for Contractor

*Note: If you are a contractor:

Your company should have a similar program in place to prevent malaria. Contact your company medical professional to obtain information, advice and preventive medication to protect you against malaria infection during your trip. You should also have the medical provider review your immunizations to ensure that all recommended vaccines for your destination have been offered and are current. If you have any further questions or concerns, please contact your employer or your ExxonMobil Contract Administrator.
